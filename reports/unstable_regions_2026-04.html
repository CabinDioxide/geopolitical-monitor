📋 全球风险概览

根据最新地缘政治分析，全球正处于二战以来并发冲突最密集的时期。当前形势的关键特征包括：

  • 并发冲突规模：全球范围内同时进行的主要地缘政治冲突达到历史高位，涉及多个战略要地
  • 地缘经济对抗：经济制裁、技术脱钩、供应链重组已成为国际竞争的首要风险因素
  • 战略资源争夺：关键矿产、能源、芯片等战略物资的控制权成为大国博弈的焦点
  • 核风险升温：核国家在地区冲突中的参与度提高，相关风险级别上升
  • 人道主义危机：多个区域的冲突导致大规模难民流动和人道主义灾难

本报告重点分析五个关键不稳定区域，评估各地区风险等级、冲突驱动因素，并提供趋势研判。

📋 Global Risk Overview

According to the latest geopolitical analysis, the world is experiencing the densest period of concurrent conflicts since World War II. Key characteristics of the current situation include:

  • Scale of Concurrent Conflicts: Multiple major geopolitical conflicts occurring simultaneously across the globe have reached historical levels
  • Geoeconomic Confrontation: Economic sanctions, technological decoupling, and supply chain restructuring have become the primary risk factors in international competition
  • Strategic Resource Competition: Control of critical minerals, energy, and semiconductor technology has become the focus of great power competition
  • Rising Nuclear Risk: Increased involvement of nuclear states in regional conflicts, with elevated risk levels
  • Humanitarian Crisis: Conflicts in multiple regions are causing large-scale refugee flows and humanitarian disasters

This report analyzes five key unstable regions, assesses risk levels and conflict drivers in each area, and provides strategic outlook assessments.

  • Semiconductor Industry Chain: Taiwan's semiconductor industry accounts for over 60% of global production capacity; the Taiwan Strait situation directly affects global technology supply chain stability
  • Energy Corridor: Key shipping lane for Middle Eastern oil and natural gas, affecting Asia-Pacific energy security

  • Slowing Russian Offensive: Russian daily advance rate decreased from 5.7 square miles/day to 4.1 square miles/day, indicating weakening offensive momentum
  • War of Attrition Mode: The conflict has evolved into a high-intensity war of attrition with both sides experiencing mounting personnel and material losses

  • Refugee Crisis: Over 6 million Ukrainians displaced, creating Europe's largest refugee crisis
  • Energy Infrastructure Destruction: Two-thirds of Ukraine's energy infrastructure destroyed, threatening civilians with inadequate heating and electricity during winters

Peace Negotiation Prospects

  • Core Disagreements: Questions regarding Ukrainian territorial integrity, NATO expansion, and war reparations remain contentious

🇸🇩 Sudan & Horn of Africa CRITICAL RISK

Sudan Civil War Status

Sudan is experiencing the worst humanitarian crisis since 2023, with conflict between the Sudanese Armed Forces (SAF) and the Rapid Support Forces (RSF).

Scale of Conflict and Casualties

  • Duration of War: Since its outbreak in April 2023, the conflict has lasted three years, becoming the world's longest ongoing conflict
  • Death Toll Estimates: Official data shows over 400,000 deaths; actual figures may be higher (estimated up to 500,000)
  • Internal Displacement: Approximately 8.8 million people forced to flee their homes, becoming the world's largest internally displaced population
  • Cross-Border Refugees: Over 4 million Sudanese refugees have fled to neighboring countries (Egypt, Chad, South Sudan, etc.), burdening regional nations

Humanitarian Disaster

  • Famine Crisis: With Sudan's total population of approximately 33.5 million, an estimated 33.7 million people (about two-thirds of the population) require humanitarian aid
  • Healthcare Collapse: Most hospitals destroyed or closed; widespread disease transmission, particularly cholera, malaria, and other infectious diseases
  • Child Crisis: Millions of children facing malnutrition, loss of education, and threat of violence
  • Surge in Sexual Violence: Conflict has caused dramatic increase in sexual violence against women and children

Risk of Regional Spillover

  • Chad Closes Eastern Border: In February 2026, Chad closed its eastern border with Sudan to prevent mass refugee influx, indicating serious threats to neighboring regions
  • Egypt Under Pressure: Egypt has accepted over 500,000 Sudanese refugees with severely inadequate domestic resources
  • Regional Instability Spread: Conflict may spread to Ethiopia, Somalia, and other Horn of Africa countries, causing major regional upheaval
